Call for Papers

Paper submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit in the format specified in the Springer Paper Template electronically in pdf or doc formats to the mail id (Paper Submission Link:). All submissions must be in English only and must be plagiarism free.

The Springer paper template can be downloaded from the link Springer Format (https://www.springer.com/gp/authors-editors/conference-proceedings/conference-proceedings-guidelines).

All submissions will strictly go through a plagiarism check, only those submissions which pass through plagiarism check will undergo a double-blind peer-review process, coordinated by the Technical Program Committee. Therefore, the authors are required to preserve the anonymity of submission (do not include author's name and affiliation while submitting the paper).

Only the accepted papers which are presented during the conference will be eligible for submission to Springer for Publication. Select papers from the conference will be published by Springer as a proceedings book volume. Springer will conduct quality checks on the accepted papers and only papers that pass these checks will be published and made available through SpringerLink Digital Library, one of the worldâ€™s largest scientific libraries. Proceedings will be submitted for inclusion to the leading indexing services: Scopus, Google Scholar and major indexing services for inclusion.

Any one of the Authors who will also be the presenter should register for the conference using the following link:

Tracks

Track 1: RF CIRCUITS and WIRELESS SYSTEMS

  • Building blocks and complete solutions at RF, mm-Wave and THz frequencies for receivers, transmitters, frequency synthesizers, transceivers, SoCs, and SiPs.
  • Innovative circuit-level and system-architecture solutions for established wireless standards and future systems or applications such as radar, sensing, and imaging.
  • Passive Components and Antennas
  • Drone-based RFID technologies
  • Novel machine learning/AI paradigm and knowledge-based methods to microwave design
  • Smart Materials and Structures, Energy Harvesting and Power Management
  • Wearable and bio-implantable wireless devices
  • Experimental setups

  • Energy efficiency, energy harvesting and wirelessly powered IoT

  • MIMO systems (massive-, multiuser-, cooperative, coordinated, cell-free, IAB, etc.) Aerial Communications
  • Quantum Communications & Computing

  • Machine-to-machine, device-to-device communications
  • Molecular, Biological and Multi-Scale Communications
  • Satellite & Space Communications
  • Testbed and prototype implementation of Wireless services
  • Smart Grid Communications
  • Optical Networks & Systems

Track 3: Signal Processing

  • Machine and Deep Learning for wireless communications, Networking and Computing
  • Sparse signal processing for wireless communications
  • Digital front-end signal processing in radio transceivers
  • Signal Processing over graphs and Networks
  • Optimization methods in signal processing
  • Analog and mixed signal processing
  • Image and video signal processing

  • Machine and Deep Learning for Speech and Audio Signal Processing, Natural Language Processing, Remote Sensing Applications and Healthcare Applications
  • Intelligent signal processing (ISP) algorithms and autonomous systems
  • Design and implementation of signal processing systems, Algorithm-to-architecture transformation
  • Special applications of signal processing

Track 4: Next Generation Networks
